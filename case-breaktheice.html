SOLA Sparkling Water · 45-second brand film

Break the Ice.

A nature thriller for twenty seconds. A soft-drink ad for the rest. Shot entirely with AI. Directed entirely by hand.

45s · 16:9Original spec brandConcept → script → direction → edit: Everflo

The idea

One title, three meanings.

The title is the script. Every scene in the film exists to pay off one of its three readings.

1Break the ice, literally

A polar bear smashes a frozen ridge apart with one blow.

2The product device

The prize inside: a can of SOLA, served ice-cold by nature itself.

3The icebreaker

Two strangers who got off on the wrong foot, sharing a drink.

The structure

Sold as a thriller. Paid off as a joke.

Forty-five seconds built on one misdirection: the audience is shown a predator. They're actually watching a customer.

Act I — The LieEverything a horror film would do: fog, silence, a charge.
Act II — The TurnOne smash flips the genre.
Act III — The TruthHe just wanted a cold one.

The product stays invisible until second 20. No logo, no can, no brand color while the lie holds — restraint most AI video never attempts, and the reason the reveal lands.

The craft

AI is the camera. Direction is the product.

Anyone can generate a bear. Keeping the same bear, the same man and the same geography across twelve shots — that's direction.

Cast like a real shoot

Four actors auditioned for the fisherman. The bear got a full character sheet — down to a notch scar on his left ear. One performer per role, held consistent through every shot in the film.

Light performs the story

Overcast dread while the lie holds. The sun breaks exactly when the truth does. The weather is a character with an arc — planned in the script, not found in the render.

Written rules, every shot

Scale anchors, locked screen direction, one action per cut, documentary light only. A written rulebook governs every generation — the discipline that makes twelve AI shots feel like one film.

Camera locked at can height One vector: paw → boot, never reversed Scale anchor: can = half a paw-width
